Goal 1/8

Define the primary aim: determining an object, explaining an event, identifying a place, instructing a skill, creating a quiz, or providing information.

Context 2/8

Provide background information and details that frame the outcome relative to a specific topic, subject, or population.

Audience 3/8

Identify intended recipients: age, experience level, ability, life circumstances, or expertise.

Tone 4/8

Specify emotional tone: formal, casual, funny, sarcastic, empathetic, technical, etc.

Style 5/8

Describe output format: table, email, report, poem, code, list, diagram, slide, etc.

Creativity Level 6/8

Set creativity from 0 (factual only) to 1 (highly imaginative). Affects response originality and risk.

Constraints 7/8

List mandatory rules: word limits, excluded content, required accuracy, style restrictions, etc.

Outcome Specifications 8/8

Define exact format details: number of bullet points, voice (active/passive), structure, technical requirements.

Element Reference

Goal
What it does:
Anchors the entire prompt. Vague goals produce vague outputs.
Strong example:
"Explain the evolutionary advantage of bilateral symmetry in early vertebrates" (specific, bounded)
Context
What it does:
Constrains the answer to relevant domain and prevents tangents.
Strong example:
"For a biology course on evolutionary developmental biology, assuming students know basic genetics"
Audience
What it does:
Controls complexity, terminology, and depth of explanation.
Strong example:
"Third-year biology majors with experience in molecular genetics but no prior knowledge of evo-devo"
Tone
What it does:
Sets voice and emotional register. Affects readability and engagement.
Strong example:
"Direct, technical, no padding. Assume reader values precision over comfort."
Style
What it does:
Determines how information is organized and presented.
Strong example:
"Numbered steps with code blocks; include visual ASCII diagram of process"
Creativity
What it does:
Balances factual accuracy with imaginative synthesis. Higher = more risk of hallucination.
Safe range:
0.0–0.3 (factual), 0.4–0.6 (balanced), 0.7–1.0 (creative)
Constraints
What it does:
Hard limits that prevent unwanted outputs. These are non-negotiable.
Strong example:
"No speculation, no brand names, cite sources, max 500 words, active voice only"
Outcome Specifications
What it does:
Defines exact structural requirements to ensure usable output.
Strong example:
"Deliver as 5 bullet points, each under 30 words, in order of importance"
